# Successful Software Engineer II Tech Resumes: What Actually Works

**Mid-level software engineering resumes that secure interviews at top companies follow dramatically different formulas depending on company type.** FAANG prioritizes scale and technical sophistication with 70-85% quantified bullets, startups demand 0-to-1 ownership stories compressed into strict one-page formats, and enterprises require ATS-optimized, process-oriented language emphasizing collaboration over individual heroics. Based on analysis of 50+ successful resumes, hiring manager quotes, and 2024-2025 placement data, this report reveals exactly what works for each category.

The competitive reality: FAANG acceptance rates sit below 1%, 75% of consulting hopefuls fail at resume screening, and 98.8% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS systems that auto-reject 75% of software resumes before human review. Understanding category-specific patterns isn't optional—it's the difference between landing interviews and disappearing into automated rejection queues.

## FAANG/MAMAA: Scale, metrics, and technical depth

FAANG resumes succeed through **relentless quantification** (80-100% of bullets include metrics), **scale demonstration** (millions of users, billions of records), and **technical sophistication signals**. The Google X-Y-Z formula—"Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]"—dominates successful examples.

### Text structure that passes the 7-second test

Recruiters spend exactly **7 seconds on average** reviewing resumes, with less than 1% of FAANG applicants receiving offers. This brutal math demands instant impact. Successful FAANG resumes follow a rigid structure: **85-90% use one page for candidates with less than 5 years experience**, expanding to two pages only when every line demonstrates significant impact. The typical bullet allocation shows 4-6 bullets for current roles, decreasing to 2-4 for previous positions, and condensing older experience to single lines.

**Concrete example from Amazon SDE hire (Kyle DeGuzman):**
```
Senior Software Engineer, Microsoft (2019-2023)
• Led design and development of enterprise microservice applications driving $35.3B annual revenue
• Reduced API latency by 80% (10s to 2s) through machine learning optimization
• Integrated unified user-agreements framework across 3 platforms, migrating 2.5B records with zero downtime
• Mentored team of 6 engineers, improving sprint velocity by 23%
```

Visual hierarchy follows strict conventions: **bold role titles** (not company names), single-column layouts only (multi-column breaks ATS), standard fonts (Times New Roman, Calibri, Arial at 11-12pt), and 0.5-inch margins. Section ordering places Skills immediately after Contact Info, providing the technical snapshot recruiters seek in those critical opening seconds, followed by Work Experience, Projects (if relevant), and Education at the bottom unless you're a recent graduate.

### Personalization through strategic keyword engineering

Kyle DeGuzman's successful Amazon application reveals the personalization strategy: "I applied to three different Amazon positions: Front-End Engineer, AWS Front-End Engineer, and Software Engineer. For every single one of those positions, I tailored my resume to match the job requirements." He extracted keywords directly from job descriptions—Java, MVC, Jest, API, REST, JavaScript, AJAX, React—and strategically wove them into experience bullets.

Company-specific terminology matters. Meta values framing around "What were you doing on your very best day at work?" Amazon emphasizes ownership and AWS keywords. Google expects the X-Y-Z formula with data-driven decision making. The Tech Interview Handbook recommends using tools like Online-Utility.org Text Analyzer to identify repeated keywords in job descriptions and optimize frequency according to importance.

### Tone: Confidence without modesty

"People love modesty, but they do not hire it," explains PirateKing, who secured offers from Google, Microsoft, and Amazon. "When applying for a job, it's not the time to be humble. Show these guys what you're made of." FAANG resumes demand **active, ownership-driven language**: Led, Spearheaded, Architected, Founded (leadership tier); Implemented, Developed, Built, Designed (execution tier); Reduced, Increased, Improved, Optimized, Achieved (impact tier).

**Weak vs Strong transformation:**
- ❌ "Worked with NLP and research work related to image processing"
- ✅ "Built cost-effective face detection API achieving 92% accuracy with 5% false positive rate, replacing third-party service and saving $250K+ annually"

Avoid passive constructions ("was responsible for," "assisted with," "participated in") that dilute individual contribution. FAANG hiring managers seek clear attribution of impact.

### Quantification: The 80% rule in action

**Target: 80-100% of bullets should include quantifiable metrics.** Successful FAANG resumes average 70-85% quantified bullets in practice. "Words can tell a story but numbers tell it way better," notes Cody, ex-Google recruiter now at LinkedIn. The hierarchy of valued metrics:

**Performance optimization examples:**
- "Reduced API latency by 80% (from 10s to 2s) through caching layer implementation"
- "Optimized database queries reducing page load time by 46ms, improving user engagement by 12%"
- "Decreased server response time by 30% implementing machine learning optimization"

**User impact scale:**
- "Built Android app enabling 10K+ party-goers to collaboratively vote on music queues"
- "Launched feature increasing daily content posted by +0.6% and monetizable views by +1.2% across 6 platforms for 3.2B MAU"
- "Developed full-stack AI quiz generator serving 7.6K+ projects on GitHub"

**Business impact:**
- "Led design of microservice applications driving $35.3 billion annual revenue"
- "Migrated to new identity-verification vendor, reducing annual costs by estimated $3M"
- "Increased email subscriber list from 80 to 200 in six months (150% growth)"

**Team and scale:**
- "Managed team of 32 engineers with 91% retention rate"
- "Led migration affecting 2.5 billion user records with zero downtime"
- "Grew Hunter College tech club from baseline to 500 members through LeetCode workshops"

The pattern: Always include **absolute numbers** (10s to 2s), **percentage improvements** (80% reduction), and **scale indicators** (2.5B records, 3.2B MAU) to demonstrate impact magnitude.

### Technical skills: Strategic organization

The most common successful format organizes by category rather than alphabetically:
```
TECHNICAL SKILLS
Languages: Python, Java, JavaScript, TypeScript, C++
Frameworks: React, Node.js, Spring Boot, Django, Flask
Databases: PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Redis, Cassandra
Tools: Docker, Kubernetes, AWS, Git, Jenkins, Terraform
```

**Critical omissions:** Microsoft Word/Excel (dilutes resume), proficiency levels ("Beginner"/"Intermediate"), HTML/CSS for backend roles unless relevant, and personal spoken languages unless 3+ languages at native/professional level.

### Storytelling through career progression

Mid-level FAANG resumes must demonstrate **escalating scope and responsibility**. The strongest applicants "show how they've taken an idea, built a plan, delivered value beyond their team, and drove it across the organization," according to FAANG hiring managers. The progression narrative:

- **Early career**: Technical depth, learning, contributions to team projects
- **Mid-level** (SE II): Ownership of features, cross-team impact, mentorship
- **Senior track signals**: Architecture, strategy, organizational influence

**Google's X-Y-Z formula applied:**
"Improved database query performance by 25% [X] through indexed searches and optimization [Z], reducing average response time to 120ms [Y]"

The STAR technique (Situation-Task-Action-Result) appears implicitly: Identify the situation ("Legacy telemetry dashboard had poor signal-to-noise ratio"), describe action ("Revised dashboard architecture and metrics"), quantify results (should include specific % improvement).

### Summary sections: The controversial element

**Majority view (against summaries):** "Putting 'Experienced engineer passionate about making great products' doesn't tell me anything. It wastes both space and seconds of the recruiter's time," says Cody. Most successful examples omit summary sections entirely, letting experience bullets speak immediately.

**Alternative approach (for summaries):** When used, effective summaries contain 2-3 bullets:
- Years of relevant experience (clear and upfront)
- 1-2 most impressive quantified achievements
- Technical focus area

Example: "6+ years building scalable backend systems for fintech, processing $10M+ daily transactions • Led teams of 5-8 engineers, shipped 12+ major features with 99.9% uptime • Technical focus: distributed systems, Python, AWS, microservices architecture"

### ATS optimization remains critical

Despite FAANG's technical sophistication, **75% of software resumes are rejected at ATS stage**. Requirements: PDF created from Word/Google Docs (text must be highlightable), standard fonts only, avoid tables/text boxes/headers/footers/columns/images, test by copy-pasting into .txt file to verify readability.

Kyle DeGuzman notes that Amazon recruiters "read EVERY application themselves" rather than relying on auto-rejection based on keywords, but ATS still parses resume content for initial categorization.

### Company-specific nuances

**Google**: Values "Awards & Leadership" section, education first for recent grads, emphasizes data-driven decision making and ethical technology considerations.

**Meta**: Frame around impact stories, emphasize moving fast with bold bets, open source contributions highly valued (React, Jest, etc.).

**Amazon**: Must align with Leadership Principles (Ownership, Deliver Results), AWS/cloud experience heavily weighted, frugality shown through cost savings.

**Microsoft**: Growth mindset language, collaboration across teams, Azure certifications valuable, diversity & inclusion contributions welcome.

**Apple**: Design thinking focus, "Think Different" innovation signals, privacy/security emphasis, extremely high bar for polish.

## Startups and unicorns: Speed, ownership, and 0-to-1 narratives

Startup resumes succeed through **demonstrating rapid execution** (always include timeframes), **business impact over scale** (revenue, cost, retention), and **scrappiness signals** showing ability to thrive with limited resources. The fundamental difference: startups hire for what you'll figure out, not what you already know.

### One page, non-negotiable

"Keep it to one page. Startup recruiters are time-starved. No recruiter is going to spend hours trying to decipher your resume," states Ryan Choi, Y Combinator director. **Unlike FAANG's flexible 1-2 page standard, startups demand strict one-page discipline.** The format combines skills upfront (technical snapshot) with chronological experience, creating impact-first ordering where most impressive achievements appear at the top.

Anthropic's hiring page explicitly states: "If you've done interesting independent research, written an insightful blog post, or made substantial contributions to open-source software, put that at the top of your resume!" This inverts traditional chronological ordering in favor of immediate impact demonstration.

### Startup-style bullets: The transformation

**Zero-to-one focus transformation:**
- ❌ FAANG style: "Developed features for payment processing system with 10M+ users"
- ✅ Startup style: "Built payment processing system from scratch handling $2M+ monthly transactions, reducing payment failures from 8% to 0.3% through custom retry logic and fraud detection"

**Cross-functional impact:**
- ❌ FAANG: "Collaborated with product team on feature specifications"
- ✅ Startup: "Shipped MVP in 6 weeks by wearing multiple hats—designed UI mockups, wrote backend APIs, conducted 15 user interviews, and deployed to production solo"

**Speed and iteration (from Uber engineer):**
✅ "Rewrote Rider app from scratch in <4 months with 3-person team, reducing crash rate by 40% and improving app rating from 3.2 to 4.5 stars"

**Business-focused metrics:**
✅ "Optimized database queries reducing server costs by $15K/month and API response time from 2.3s to 400ms, directly improving user retention by 18%"

**Autonomy and ownership:**
✅ "Identified critical security vulnerability in authentication flow, led cross-team effort to patch 8 microservices, and implemented monitoring to prevent future incidents—all within 72 hours"

**Full-stack capabilities:**
✅ "Architected and deployed end-to-end feature allowing real-time collaboration: React frontend, Node.js backend, WebSocket infrastructure, and AWS deployment with 99.9% uptime"

**Scrappiness signal:**
✅ "Implemented streamlined sales process leveraging CRM tools and automation, resulting in 30% reduction in lead-to-close time and 150% revenue growth in first year"

**Early-stage impact:**
✅ "As employee #12, established engineering best practices including CI/CD pipelines, code review standards, and on-call rotation that scaled to 50-person engineering team"

### Personalization through mission alignment

Anthropic's stated values—"Act for the global good," "Be helpful, honest, and harmless," "Put the mission first"—must appear in resume language. Effective demonstrations:

✅ In summary: "Software engineer passionate about building reliable, interpretable AI systems that benefit humanity"

✅ In bullets: "Prioritized user safety by implementing content filtering system that reduced harmful outputs by 85% while maintaining response quality"

**Cultural fit signals:** Use "shipped," "launched," "built from scratch," "pivoted," "iterated rapidly." Avoid corporate-speak like "synergized," "facilitated alignment," "drove strategic initiatives."

The Pragmatic Engineer (Uber/Skyscanner veteran) emphasizes: "Being an owner and stepping up to proactively fix things was not just something nice to have: it was the path to succeeding at places like these. Autonomy and ownership were not limited to software development."

### Tone: Builder mindset over process adherence

Startups want to see **the human behind the resume**. The tone shifts from FAANG's professional formality to enthusiastic action orientation:

- "Architected and launched" (not "was responsible for building")
- "Prototyped and validated" (shows experimentation)
- "Experimented with 3 approaches, selected optimal solution" (reveals thinking process)
- "Self-taught [technology] to unblock project deadline" (demonstrates initiative)

Y Combinator hiring managers value "horsepower, work ethic, growth mindset, and an instinct to take initiative." The language must convey these attributes explicitly.

### Metrics: Business impact over scale

**FAANG pattern:** "Improved system serving 100M+ users"

**Startup pattern:** "Increased conversion rate from 2.1% to 3.8%, generating $400K additional ARR"

The five metrics startups prioritize:
1. **Revenue impact**: "$X added, $Y saved" (most valued)
2. **Speed**: "shipped in X weeks, reduced time by Y%"
3. **User metrics**: "retention +X%, engagement +Y%"
4. **Cost efficiency**: "reduced costs by $X"
5. **Team velocity**: "enabled team to ship X% faster"

LinkedIn startup expert Jeremy Schifeling: "Quantify the accomplishment: List specific, numerical outcomes that came directly from your work... Brand the accomplishment: Even if you don't have a specific number to share, you can increase your bullet's impressiveness by dropping a fancy brand name or title."

### Technical vs soft skills: Full-stack expectation

Startups seek **breadth over depth** at mid-level, expecting engineers to wear multiple hats. Anthropic explicitly states: "Engineers here do lots of research, and researchers do lots of engineering... all of our papers have engineers as authors, and often as first author."

**Recommended skills section:**
```
Technical: React, Node.js, Python, PostgreSQL, AWS, Docker, REST APIs
Product: A/B testing, user interviews, analytics (Amplitude/Mixpanel)
Collaboration: Led 3-person team, mentored 2 junior engineers
```

This structure signals comfort beyond pure coding—product thinking and collaboration capabilities that enable startup velocity.

**Cross-functional demonstration:**
✅ "Collaborated with design, sales, and customer success to scope MVP features based on user feedback from 30+ interviews"
✅ "Partnered with data science team to implement ML recommendation engine, improving click-through rate by 35%"

### Storytelling: 0-to-1 project narratives

**Ambiguity to clarity story:**
✅ "Company had no engineering standards; researched best practices, proposed tech stack (React/Node/Postgres), got buy-in from founders, and implemented foundation used by team for 2+ years"

**Rapid iteration narrative:**
✅ "Initial feature had 5% adoption; conducted 10 user interviews, identified pain points, shipped 3 iterations in 2 weeks, increased adoption to 40%"

**Scrappiness example:**
✅ "Needed payment processing but budget was $0; integrated Stripe API over weekend, launched beta with 50 users, validated concept before company invested in infrastructure"

These narratives demonstrate **comfort with ambiguity**, **rapid execution**, and **resourcefulness**—the trinity of startup engineering success.

### Summary: Mission-driven enthusiasm

❌ FAANG style: "Software Engineer with 3 years experience in distributed systems and scalable architectures"

✅ Startup style: "Full-stack engineer who ships fast. Built 3 products from 0→1, comfortable with ambiguity, and passionate about turning user problems into elegant solutions."

✅ Mission-driven version: "Engineer with track record of rapid execution in early-stage environments. Joined startup as employee #8, scaled product to 100K users while maintaining 99.9% uptime. Excited by hard problems in AI safety."

Ryan Choi's guidance: "Create a tailored two-sentence byline about what you do and why you're excited about that startup." The formula: years + expertise + startup mindset + domain enthusiasm.

### Side projects and open source: Essential, not optional

Anthropic's explicit guidance: "If you've done interesting independent research, written an insightful blog post, or made substantial contributions to open-source software, put that at the top of your resume!"

**Effective projects section:**
```
Personal Projects:
• DevTools CLI - Open-source developer productivity tool (2.5K GitHub stars)
  - Used by engineering teams at Stripe, Figma, and 40+ other companies
  - Reduced build times by 60% through parallel execution and caching
  
• Weekend Project: Built AI code reviewer using GPT-4 API
  - Achieved 78% accuracy in detecting bugs vs manual review
  - Gained 500+ beta users through Product Hunt launch
```

For mid-level engineers targeting startups, **side projects aren't bonuses—they're proof of builder mindset** essential for screening success.

### Statistical insights: The startup hiring reality

**Anthropic hiring data:** About 50% of technical staff have PhDs; 50% don't. About 50% had prior ML experience; 50% didn't. "We have several brilliant colleagues who never went to college." This reveals **impact demonstration trumps credentials**.

**Hiring speed:** Average time from application to interview is 1 week (versus 2-4 weeks at FAANG), with 3-4 interview rounds (versus 5-7), and more direct founder/hiring manager contact.

**Platform insights:** Lenny's Newsletter research shows AngelList/Wellfound was "by far the most mentioned job board" for finding early startup employees. Early startup hires came from public job boards, GitHub contributions, and employee networks—not traditional recruiting pipelines.

## Enterprise companies: Process, compliance, and ATS mastery

Enterprise resumes succeed through **strict ATS optimization** (98.8% of Fortune 500 use ATS that reject 75% of resumes), **business value metrics** (cost savings, efficiency, compliance), and **collaborative framing** (60% team-oriented versus FAANG's 30%). The fundamental reality: software engineering in large organizations prioritizes reliability, process adherence, and stakeholder management over technical innovation.

### Format: ATS compatibility is non-negotiable

**98.8% of Fortune 500 companies use Applicant Tracking Systems**, including Workday Recruiting (IBM, Amazon, Visa, Cisco, Accenture, Goldman Sachs, Microsoft, Oracle, Johnson & Johnson, Boeing, PepsiCo, General Electric), Taleo (automotive to public sector), and SAP SuccessFactors (multi-industry standard). Goldman Sachs recruiter Sumeya Ibrahim: "Design features like pictures, columns, photos, etc. can prevent ATS systems from correctly scanning your resume."

**Non-negotiable format requirements:**
- **One-column layout only** (multi-column breaks parsing)
- **Standard fonts**: Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman (10-12pt)
- **Black and white only** (no graphics, colors, photos, logos)
- **Standard section headings**: "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills" (creative alternatives confuse ATS)
- **Reverse chronological format strongly preferred** (functional formats fail)
- **PDF or DOCX** (verify posting requirements)
- **No headers/footers** (content gets lost in parsing)
- **Strict 1 page for <10 years experience**, 2 pages acceptable for senior roles

Jobscan research finding: "Resumes with the exact job title from the job posting in them were 10.6 times more likely to get an interview."

### Text structure and bullet organization

Enterprise bullet allocation mirrors FAANG patterns but with stronger emphasis on older experience if relevant to target role. Typical structure: 4-6 bullets for current role, 3-5 for previous positions, 2-3 for older relevant experience. Unlike startups that ruthlessly condense, enterprises value stability signals from sustained career progression.

**Actual IBM Software Engineer example:**
```
Software Engineer, IBM (2020-Present)
• Designed and implemented scalable software solutions to meet customer business requirements efficiently
• Collaborated with cross-functional teams to define software requirements and technical specifications
• Conducted code reviews to ensure adherence to coding standards and best practices
• Led Agile/Scrum ceremonies for 12-person development team, improving sprint velocity by 18%
```

Note the language: "collaborated," "cross-functional," "standards," "best practices"—process-oriented terminology that signals corporate compatibility.

### Personalization: Industry-specific value signals

**Enterprise value language patterns:**
- **Process adherence**: "Implemented Agile methodologies," "Following SDLC phases," "Adhering to compliance standards"
- **Stability emphasis**: "Maintained," "Supported," "Ensured reliability," "Achieved 99.9% uptime"
- **Cross-functional collaboration**: "Coordinated with stakeholders," "Collaborated cross-functionally," "Aligned with business requirements"
- **Compliance and governance**: "Ensured GDPR compliance," "Maintained SOC 2 certification," "Implemented security protocols"

**Finance sector specifics (JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs):**

Wall Street Oasis guidance emphasizes "Strong Names"—recognizing PE/banking/HR universe brands signals you passed their interview processes. Goldman Sachs recruiter Alex Thompson: "Quantify your achievements. Make sure you are putting your achievements in there with the key results. You need to show what you have actually delivered in your roles."

JPMorgan career advice: "Avoid buzzwords and jargon from other industries. Don't just list responsibilities." The tension: demonstrate technical capability while speaking business language leadership understands.

**Consulting firm formalization (Accenture, Deloitte):**

McKinsey/BCG/Bain standards applicable to consulting-style enterprises: 25% of bullets demonstrate analytical capabilities, 25% show initiative and results, 25% highlight innovation, 25% demonstrate collaboration. The section ordering differs from tech companies: Education (if recent grad) OR Experience first, then Work Experience, Extracurricular Achievements (critical for demonstrating well-roundedness), Additional Skills (languages, certifications).

### Tone: Conservative professional with team orientation

**Enterprise tone characteristics:**
- **Formal but not stuffy**: Professional without being robotic
- **Team-oriented framing**: "Collaborated to achieve," "Led cross-functional team," "Mentored junior engineers"
- **Business outcome focus**: Less about technical elegance, more about business value
- **Risk mitigation emphasis**: "Reduced downtime," "Improved reliability," "Ensured compliance"

**The 60/40 rule:** Enterprise resumes frame 60% of achievements collaboratively, 40% individually—the inverse of startup's 30/70 split toward individual contribution.

**Example bullets showing team framing:**
- "Coordinated with software engineering team to develop custom applications for enterprise customers valued at $700K"
- "Collaborated with cross-functional teams to define software requirements and technical specifications"
- "Led a team of 5 engineers to rebuild the payment processing system, reducing transaction failures by 40%"

IGotAnOffer consulting guide: "The content of your resume should stand out, not its format." This philosophy permeates enterprise hiring—substance over style, results over innovation narratives.

### Metrics: Cost savings and efficiency dominate

**Enterprise metrics hierarchy:**

1. **Cost savings** (most valued): "Saved $500K annually through vendor contract renegotiation," "Reduced operational costs by 30% while improving system performance by 40%," "Automated testing processes to save over $100,000 in annual labor costs"

2. **Efficiency improvements**: "Reduced deployment cycle time by 60%," "Improved project delivery times by 5%," "Decreased bug reports by 30% through code review implementation"

3. **Compliance & risk reduction**: "Achieved 100% compliance with GDPR and CCPA regulations," "Reduced security incidents by 85% through zero-trust framework," "Ensured data integrity with zero downtime during migration"

4. **Scale of systems supported**: "Supported 20 million insurees through EHR system," "Managed 400TB+ data improving availability by 30%," "Scaled system to handle 300% traffic increase during peak events"

5. **Process improvements**: "Implemented Agile methodologies resulting in 20% team efficiency increase," "Reduced validation cycle time by 30% saving $500K annually," "Streamlined workflows using Lean Six Sigma resulting in 32% cycle time reduction"

### ATS optimization techniques (enterprise critical)

**Keyword optimization strategy:**
1. Extract exact keywords from job description—use job title verbatim
2. Include both acronyms and full terms: "ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)"
3. Match technology names exactly: "Oracle Database 12c" not "Oracle DB"
4. Use industry standard terminology: "Agile/Scrum" not "agile development"
5. Avoid creative section headings: Use "Work Experience" not "My Journey"

Goldman Sachs recruiter Alex Thompson on tailoring: "Tailoring your CV is 'absolutely fundamental.' Put yourself in the shoes of the person hiring for the role. Think about what's important to them when they're making decisions. You can see when individuals do that, and it really does jump out."

**Enterprise ATS systems require:**
- PDF created from Word/Google Docs (text highlightable)
- Standard fonts only (Times New Roman, Arial, Calibri, Helvetica)
- Avoid tables, text boxes, headers/footers, columns, images, graphics, icons
- Test: Copy-paste into .txt file—if readable, ATS can parse it

## Cross-category comparison: Strategic differentiation

Understanding where these categories diverge reveals how to tailor effectively for target company types.

### Format and structure differences

| Element | FAANG | Startups | Enterprise |

### Tone and language comparison

**FAANG preferred verbs:** Built, Created, Architected, Designed, Innovated, Pioneered, Scaled, Optimized, Launched

**Startup preferred verbs:** Shipped, Founded, Built from scratch, Launched, Iterated, Validated, Experimented, Proved

**Startups emphasize:** Speed, ownership, scrappiness, business impact, 0-to-1 building, resourcefulness

**Enterprise emphasizes:** Stability, reliability, process, compliance, collaboration, efficiency, cost reduction

### Metrics priority hierarchy

**FAANG top metrics:**
1. User scale (millions, billions)
2. Performance improvements (latency, speed)
3. System scale (throughput, data volume)
4. User engagement (DAU, MAU, retention)
5. Team impact (people mentored, projects led)

**Startup top metrics:**
1. Revenue impact ($X added, $Y saved)
2. Speed (shipped in X weeks)
3. User metrics (retention, engagement %)
4. Cost efficiency (reduced costs $X)
5. Team velocity (enabled X% faster shipping)

**Enterprise top metrics:**
1. Cost savings (most valued)
2. Efficiency improvements (cycle time, productivity)
3. Compliance & risk reduction
4. Scale of systems supported
5. Process improvements (methodologies adopted)

### Team vs individual framing

**FAANG:** 30% collaborative, 70% individual achievement framing

**Startups:** 20% collaborative, 80% individual ownership emphasis

**Enterprise:** 60% collaborative, 40% individual contribution balance

This single difference dramatically changes resume tone and perceived cultural fit.

### Quantification expectations

**FAANG:** 70-85% of bullets include metrics (target 80-100%)

**Startups:** 75-90% of bullets include metrics (always with timeframes)

**Enterprise:** 60-75% of bullets include metrics (business value focus)

All categories value quantification, but the types of metrics and percentage coverage vary meaningfully.

### Skills section emphasis

**FAANG:** Technical depth, categorized by type, recent technologies prioritized, certifications optional

**Startup:** Full-stack breadth, product tools included (A/B testing, analytics), side projects essential

**Enterprise:** Proficiency levels specified, enterprise platforms highlighted, methodologies prominent, certifications separate section

### Soft skills demonstration

**FAANG:** Embedded in 20-30% of bullets, never standalone, leadership through impact

**Startups:** Demonstrated through cross-functional stories, minimal explicit mention, builder mindset implicit

**Enterprise:** Explicit emphasis in 40% of bullets, stakeholder management critical, process adherence valued

### Personalization strategies

**FAANG:** Extract keywords from job descriptions, align with company values (Google: data-driven, Meta: move fast, Amazon: ownership), demonstrate technical sophistication for scale challenges

**Startups:** Mission alignment language, cultural fit signals (scrappiness, speed), domain enthusiasm explicit, side projects showing initiative

**Enterprise:** Exact job description matching, industry-specific terminology, process methodology mentions, stability and reliability emphasis, compliance awareness

## Guideline implementation patterns: Actionable insights

Beyond understanding categories, successful resumes follow specific implementation patterns for each element.

### Quantified achievements: Percentage coverage by category

**FAANG target:** Aim for 80-100% quantified bullets, minimum acceptable 70%. Ex-Google recruiter Cody: "We have to tell our story through data and numbers. Words can tell a story but numbers tell it way better."

**Startup target:** Aim for 75-90% quantified bullets, always include timeframes. Y Combinator: "What impact did you have? This is particularly important for product engineers—it shows your head is in the right space for a startup!"

**Enterprise target:** Aim for 60-75% quantified bullets, emphasize business value. Goldman Sachs recruiter: "Make sure you are putting your achievements in there with the key results."

**Implementation:** For each experience bullet, ask: What changed? By how much? How quickly? What was the business/user/technical impact?

### Action verb usage patterns

**Strong action verb distribution:**
- **30-40%**: Impact verbs (Reduced, Increased, Improved, Optimized, Achieved, Delivered)
- **30-40%**: Creation verbs (Built, Developed, Designed, Architected, Implemented, Created)
- **20-30%**: Leadership verbs (Led, Spearheaded, Directed, Managed, Mentored, Coordinated)
- **10-20%**: Analysis verbs (Analyzed, Identified, Researched, Investigated)

**Avoid entirely:** Helped, Assisted, Worked on, Participated in, Contributed to, Was part of, Responsible for, Tasked with

### Achievement vs responsibility framing ratios

**FAANG:** 90% achievement-framed, 10% responsibility context

**Startups:** 95% achievement-framed, 5% responsibility (ownership implicit)

**Enterprise:** 70% achievement-framed, 30% responsibility context (process adherence valued)

**Weak (responsibility-focused):**
"Responsible for building dashboards for analytics team"

**Strong (achievement-focused):**
"Built real-time analytics dashboards enabling 15 engineers to identify issues 2x faster, reducing mean time to resolution by 40%"

### Keyword optimization approaches

**FAANG approach:** Extract 15-20 keywords from job description, include at least 10 naturally in resume, prioritize technical terms (languages, frameworks, methodologies), use tools like Online-Utility.org Text Analyzer

**Startup approach:** Mission-critical words (shipped, launched, built, 0-to-1, ownership, scrappy), domain-specific terms (AI safety for Anthropic, fintech for Stripe), cultural fit vocabulary (move fast, iterate, experiment)

**Enterprise approach:** Exact job title matching (10.6x more likely to get interview), both acronym and full term (ERP + Enterprise Resource Planning), methodology names exactly (Agile/Scrum not "agile"), compliance terms relevant to industry

## Opening strategies: Summary section decision framework

**Use summary when:**
- Changing industries/domains (contextualize experience)
- 10+ years experience (provide executive snapshot)
- Complex career path (guide reader's interpretation)
- Enterprise applications (85% use summaries)
- Multiple relevant skill areas (frontend + backend + cloud)

**Skip summary when:**
- <5 years experience (let work speak)
- Applying to startups (waste of precious one-page space)
- Linear career progression (obvious from chronology)
- Strong recent experience (first role tells story)

**Summary formula when used:**
[Title] with [X years] in [domain] | [1-2 top achievements with metrics] | [Technical expertise] | [Relevant soft skill or career goal]

Example: "Software Engineer II with 4 years in fintech | Built payment systems processing $50M+ monthly | Python, AWS, React expertise | Passionate about secure, scalable financial infrastructure"

## Closing strategies: What to include at resume end

**FAANG closing pattern:**
1. Education (degree, GPA if >3.6, relevant coursework if recent grad)
2. Awards & Leadership (only if competitive: "1 of 22 recipients")
3. Languages (only if 3+ languages at professional level)
4. Interests (only if genuinely unique or company-relevant)

**Startup closing pattern:**
1. Projects (essential—personal, open source, side projects with metrics)
2. Education (brief unless recent grad)
3. Open source contributions (GitHub stars, usage statistics)
4. Technical blog/writing (if established audience)

**Enterprise closing pattern:**
1. Education (traditional placement)
2. Certifications (prominent separate section with dates)
3. Professional memberships (IEEE, ACM, industry associations)
4. Awards & Recognition (company performance awards, patents)
5. Languages with proficiency levels (corporate citizenship valued)

**What to never include (all categories):** Objective statements, "references available upon request," full home address (city/state only), photo, birth date, marital status, religion, salary expectations

## Common pitfalls and how to avoid them

**FAANG-specific mistakes:**
- Vague scale ("large system") instead of specific numbers ("10M+ users")
- Passive voice ("was responsible for") instead of active ownership ("Built," "Led")
- Missing quantification (only 50% of bullets have metrics)
- Generic resume not tailored to company-specific values
- Ignoring GitHub/personal project links

**Startup-specific mistakes:**
- Two-page resume (instant disqualification)
- Emphasizing process over outcomes
- Corporate jargon ("synergized," "facilitated alignment")
- Missing timeframes ("shipped feature" instead of "shipped in 3 weeks")
- No side projects or open source contributions
- Failing to demonstrate 0-to-1 capabilities
- Scale metrics without business impact

**Enterprise-specific mistakes:**
- Creative formatting that breaks ATS (multi-column, graphics)
- Missing exact job title from posting
- Acronyms without spelled-out versions
- No methodology mentions (Agile, SDLC, Six Sigma)
- Individual heroics framing (60% should be collaborative)
- Missing certifications section
- Casual language tone
- Generic resume not tailored to job description
- Typos (77% rejection rate from CareerBuilder study)
